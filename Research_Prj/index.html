Censorship is not inherently good or bad. It is a governance mechanism that operates under different political, legal, and social values, deployed by states, platforms, and institutions alike in the name of protection as often as control. This atlas does not attempt to determine whether censorship is justified. Instead, it asks who is doing the justifying, on what grounds, and whether the reader would draw the same line.

03. method

on this draft

Documented component. The sixteen cases included in this draft are publicly known, verifiable instances of information restriction or moderation.

Placeholder component. The numeric position assigned to each case, and by extension to each word derived from it, along the protective to repressive scale is a rough estimate developed for this prototype rather than a sourced or defensible measurement.

A completed version of this project would require a transparent scoring method, most likely constructed from primary sources such as court records, platform transparency reports, and legislative text rather than individual judgment, and ideally weighted or contested by multiple readers rather than fixed by a single author. The taxonomy of rationale, actor, and outcome is the element most in need of feedback at this stage, particularly whether these three axes represent the appropriate framework, or whether a fourth axis, such as jurisdiction or reversibility, should be added.
